Cucumber and Avocado Sushi

For further information, please refer to the video View Video

Prep Time: 35 mins
Cook Time: 25 mins
Additional Time: 5 mins
Total Time:1 hr 5 mins
Servings: 6
Yield: 24 rolls

Ingredients

1 ¼ cups water 1 cup uncooked glutinous white rice (sushi rice)
3 tablespoons rice vinega
1 pinch salt
4 sheets nori (dry seaweed)
½ medium cucumber, sliced into thin strips
1 medium avocado - peeled, pitted and sliced

Step1: Combine water and rice in a saucepan and bring to a boil. Cover, reduce the heat to low, and simmer until rice is tender and water has been absorbed, about 20 minutes. Remove from the heat, stir in vinegar and salt, and set aside to cool for at least 5 minutes.

Step2: Cover a bamboo sushi mat with plastic wrap to keep rice from sticking. Place one nori sheet over the plastic. Spread rice evenly onto nori sheet, leaving about a 1/2 inch uncovered at the bottom. Arrange cucumber and avocado across the center of the rice. Lift the mat, roll over cucumber and avocado once, and press down. Unroll, then roll again towards the uncovered end of the nori to make a long roll. Seal roll with a little water if necessary. Repeat to make remaining rolls.

Step3: Use a sharp, wet knife to slice each roll into 6 pieces.

Nutrition Facts (per serving)

5g:Fat
29g: Carbs
3g:Protein

Paneer Tikka Recipe

Ingredients:

  • 250g Paneer (cottage cheese)
  • 1 cup Yogurt
  • 2 tbsp Tandoori masala
  • 1 tbsp Ginger-garlic paste
  • 1 tbsp Lemon juice
  • 1 tbsp Oil
  • Salt to taste
  • 1 Bell pepper (capsicum), cubed
  • 1 Onion, cubed
  • Skewers (wooden or metal)

Instructions:

  1. Prepare the Marinade: In a bowl, mix yogurt, tandoori masala, ginger-garlic paste, lemon juice, oil, and salt.
  2. Marinate the Paneer: Cut paneer into cubes and add to the marinade. Toss well to coat. Let it marinate for at least 30 minutes.
  3. Add Vegetables: Add bell pepper and onion cubes to the marinade. Mix gently.
  4. Skewer the Ingredients: Thread the marinated paneer and vegetables onto the skewers, alternating between them.
  5. Cook the Tikka: Preheat a grill or oven to 200°C (392°F). Place the skewers on the grill or a baking tray and cook for about 15-20 minutes, turning occasionally.
  6. Serve: Remove from the grill and serve hot with mint chutney and lemon wedges.
